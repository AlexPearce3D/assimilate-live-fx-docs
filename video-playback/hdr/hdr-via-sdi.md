# HDR via SDI

If you are going out SDI to the LED Processor or an HDR Monitor, you can skip all Windows and Nvidia settings. <br>

The main thing you need to make sure is that the SDI cable(s) is 6g or ideally 12g sdi (capable of carrying 10bit signal).&#x20;

In Video IO settings, make sure that format is at least YUV 10/422 (10 bit).&#x20;

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

In Settings > Monitors, make sure to set your monitor to PQ or HLG (depending on your workflow)

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

In your LED processor or TV, make sure to set the signal to be PQ or HLG (depending on your workflow).&#x20;
